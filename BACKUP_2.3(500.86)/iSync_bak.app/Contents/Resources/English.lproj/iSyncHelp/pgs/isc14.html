Synchronizing your contacts and calendars
 

Once you've added your devices to iSync, you can sync the contact and calendar information on your computer and devices. (If you haven't yet set up your devices to work with iSync, click "Tell me more" at the bottom of this page.)

IMPORTANT: You should sync your phone with only one computer. If you sync your devices with more than one computer, your information may not sync correctly (you could see duplicates or wrong information).

1.

If this is your first time syncing any of your devices on this computer, click the device's icon to see its sync settings, and choose "Erase data on device then sync" from the "For first sync" pop-up menu.

For your first sync, it's a good idea to replace the information on your devices with the information that's on your computer so that each device starts with the same information. If, however, you have some information that's stored only on a device, you can choose to merge the information on your device and computer. If you merge for your first sync, you may see what look like duplicate pieces of information. In fact, those items probably have subtle differences. For more information about how synchronization works, click "Tell me more."

For your second and subsequent syncs, the information is always merged so that any changes you make on your computer and on your device are updated in both places.

2.

Select the types of information you want to synchronize in each device's sync settings (to see a device's sync settings, click its icon).

3.

When you're ready to sync, click the Sync Devices button in the top-right corner of the iSync window.

If your devices include a Palm OS device, you'll need to perform a HotSync operation on the Palm device (or press the HotSync button on the device's cradle) in order to sync. When you perform a HotSync, iSync also synchronizes any other devices that are present.

4.

If you're synchronizing a Symbian OS phone, check your phone for messages asking if it's OK to communicate with your computer and reply "yes."

To avoid having to reply to these messages before each sync, you can authorize your computer to communicate with your phone in your phone's Bluetooth settings. For more information, see the instructions that came with your phone.

If one or more devices that you've added to iSync are not present, iSync saves your changes and updates the missing devices the next time the devices are present.

For more information about choosing which information gets synchronized, or tips on speeding up synchronization, click "Tell me more."

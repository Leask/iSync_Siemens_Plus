  What's new in iSync
 

The way synchronization works has been revised in Mac OS X version 10.4. The sync engine is now a part of the Mac OS, allowing multiple applications to use the synchronization services of the OS.

You can still use iSync to synchronize the contacts and calendars on your computer and devices (such as your mobile phone, Palm OS device, or iPod), but computer-to-computer synchronization is now handled in the .Mac System Preference.

Note: If you have iTunes 4.8 or later installed, you use iTunes, not iSync, to transfer contacts and calendars to your iPod. For more information, open iTunes and choose Help > "iTunes and Music Store Help."

Using iSync 2.0 you can:

Turn off synchronization on your computer
You can turn off synchronization completely so that no information gets synchronized across computers or devices.
Learn more

For more information about .Mac synchronization or the new sync services in Mac OS X, search Mac OS Help (choose Library > Mac Help). Or, visit the Mac OS X website at www.apple.com/macosx.

Resolving synchronization conflicts
 

If you change the same piece of information (for example, a phone number) in more than one place, Mac OS X synchronization services updates all your devices with one version of the information, and stores the other versions of that information in the Conflict Resolver.

The Conflict Resolver keeps track of the conflicts until you resolve them by choosing which information to use on your computer and devices.

Choose the Conflicts menu item from the sync menu on the right side of the menu bar, and follow the instructions that appear. (If you don't see the sync menu, choose iSync > Preferences and select the "Show status in menu bar" checkbox.)

If you see "No Conflicts" in the sync menu, the synchronized information did not contain any conflicts.

After you resolve all the conflicts, it's a good idea to sync your computer and devices again to make sure your choices are updated everywhere.

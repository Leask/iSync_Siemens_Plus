Turning off synchronization on your computer
 

You can turn off synchronization completely, so that no information gets synchronized across computers or devices. Turning off synchronization completely prevents any Mac OS X synchronization clients (such as iSync, Mail, iCal, or .Mac) from synchronizing their information. You may want to do this if you are using memory-intensive applications.

To turn off synchronization for all Mac OS X synchronization clients, choose iSync > Preferences, and deselect the "Enable syncing on this computer" checkbox.

If you disable syncing for your computer, any changes that are made while computer syncing was off are updated the next time you enable syncing on the computer.

You can also turn off syncing for a particular device (for example, your mobile phone). For more information, click "Tell me more."

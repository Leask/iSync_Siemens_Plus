I'm not seeing the information I expect after a sync
 

If your information doesn't appear to be updating correctly across devices, check the following:

•

Make sure you've set up your device to sync with only one computer. If you add a device to more than one computer, your information may not sync correctly. For information about removing a device from a computer, click "Tell me more."

•

Make sure all your devices are properly connected to (or within Bluetooth-range of) your computer. If a device was not present during a sync, it won't be updated until the next time it's present.

•

You may have synchronization conflicts that need to be resolved before you will see the expected information across all your devices. To see if you have any synchronization conflicts or to resolve them, choose iSync > Preferences and make sure the "Show status in menu bar" checkbox is selected. Then choose the Resolve Conflicts Now from the sync menu on the right side of the menu bar (if you don't have any conflicts, this menu item is not available). Follow the instructions in the Conflict Resolver to resolve the synchronization conflicts.

•

If you're seeing duplicate or wrong information after you've removed your device from iSync on one computer and added it to iSync on another computer, you need to reset the information on your device using the information on the computer you're planning to use for syncing. For more information about resetting the information on your devices, click "Tell me more."

•

If you don't see the information you expect to see after syncing, it may be because you tried to cancel a reset before it was finished. Once the reset synchronization process has begun, you cannot cancel it. Clicking the Cancel Sync button in iSync (or choosing Cancel Sync from the sync menu) only postpones the reset until the next time you sync.

•

If you've resolved sync conflicts, or changed your setup or any settings, try syncing again.

If synchronization is failing or you're seeing frequent error messages, click "Tell me more."

Setting up to sync your Bluetooth mobile phone
 

If you have a Bluetooth enabled Mac, you can use iSync to wirelessly synchronize the contact and calendar information on your computer with the contact and calendar information on your iSync-compatible Bluetooth mobile phone. (For a list of iSync-compatible devices, visist www.apple.com/isync/devices.hmtl.)

Before you can sync your phone and computer, you need to add your phone to iSync. You need to do this only once.

IMPORTANT: You should sync your phone with only one computer. If you add your phone to iSync on more than one computer, your information may not sync correctly (you could see duplicates or wrong information).

1.

Make sure your phone is in discoverable mode.

For more information, see the information that came with your mobile phone.

2.

Make sure you have a USB Bluetooth adapter connected to the computer you want to sync your phone with, or that your computer is Bluetooth enabled.

If you see a Bluetooth icon in System Preferences, your computer is Bluetooth enabled. (Choose Apple menu > System Preferences.)

For information on connecting a Bluetooth USB adapter, see the instructions that came with your Bluetooth USB adapter. For information about Bluetooth, search Mac Help (choose Library > Mac Help), or visit www.apple.com/bluetooth.

3.

Choose Apple menu > System Preferences, then click the Bluetooth icon.

4.

Click the Devices tab, then click Set Up New Device and follow the instructions that appear to pair your computer with your phone.

For more information about setting up Bluetooth, search Mac Help for Bluetooth (choose Library > Mac Help and enter your search).

5.

Open iSync.

If you can see your phone in the iSync window, you're done setting up.

6.

If you don't see your phone, in iSync, choose Devices > Add Device.

If you have a Symbian OS phone, you need to install some software on your phone before it can work with iSync. When you see a message that you need to install some software, follow the instructions that appear.

7.

Double-click the phone in the Add Device window to add it to iSync.

If you have a Symbian OS phone, each time you sync you'll need to reply to messages on your phone to allow your computer to connect to it. To avoid having to reply to these messages, you can authorize your computer to communicate with your phone in your phone's Bluetooth settings. For more information, see the instructions that came with your phone.

For instructions on syncing the contacts and calendars on your computer and phone, click "Tell me more."

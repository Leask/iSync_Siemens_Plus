iSync Help
  Tips
 

Here are some great tips and tricks to help you work faster and smarter in iSync.

Syncing

To speed up the sync between your computer and mobile phone or to limit the memory used for storing information, you can choose to sync less information. In Address Book, create a group for the contacts you want to have on your device. In iSync, click the device icon to show its sync settings, then choose only the contacts and calendars that you want to sync. For more information on choosing what information is synchronized, search iSync Help.

To help speed up the sync with a Palm OS device, make sure the HotSync Progress window is in front.

To quickly perform a sync, press the iSync icon in the Dock and choose Start Sync. If iSync is open, you can also see the date of your last sync in the Dock menu.

If you see the iSync icon on the right side of the menu bar (in any application), you can also quickly perform a sync by choosing Sync Now from the iSync status menu. To make the iSync status menu appear in the menu bar, select "Show the sync status in the menu bar" in the iSync Preferences window. You can also quickly open iSync using the iSync status menu.

Password information for private WebDAV servers is not synced. When syncing calendars that require a password (for example, calendars you've subscribed to that are published on a private WebDAV server), you will see a message asking you to reenter the password. Enter the server password to sync the calendar.

To make sure you have the latest changes on all your devices, be sure to resolve any synchronization conflicts regularly. To resolve conflicts, choose the Conflicts item from the sync menu (on the right side of the menu bar). If you don't see the sync menu, open iSync Preferences and select the "Show status in menu bar" checkbox.

If you have a Symbian OS phone, each time you sync you'll need to reply to messages on your phone to allow your computer to connect to it. To prevent these messages from appearing and always allow your computer to sync with your phone, you can set your computer as "authorized" in your phone's Bluetooth settings. For more information, see the instructions that came with your phone.

Palm OS device

If you added a Palm OS device and chose to hide the message that reminds you to click the HotSync button, you can make the message appear again by choosing iSync > Preferences and selecting the "Display Palm Hotsync warning" checkbox.

Sync history

To see detailed information about the synchronizations you've initiated from this computer, choose Window > Show Logs, and then click the triangle to the left of the sync you want to learn about.
